About US

Our lab is currently involved in genomics and transcriptomics project which are majorly engage with computational and bioinformatics based approach for analysis of DNA and RNA sequencing which includes various software for aligning and assembly the RNA-Seq data, denovo genome assembly, whole genome sequencing, whole exome sequencing for analysis. We are working with biologists to address the arising problem using computational methods and our aim is to provide computational solutions to biologists by developing a user-friendly interface and database for data visualization and analysis.

Research areas in which our lab is actively involved:

Epigenetics and genomics

Process of epigenetic inheritance includes DNA methylation, post translational modification of histone and chromatin organization plays an important role in controlling the functional outcomes in DNA sequences. We engage with ONT (Oxford Nanopore Technology), ChiP-Seq, RNA-Seq, Bisulfite Sequencing to investigate the DNA, RNA base modification and DNA encoded regulatory mechanism along with this we also perform meta-analyses using existing datasets of various genomes from public repositories.

Population Genetics

Our aim is to assess, managing and analyzing the quality datasets of population specific genetic samples. We explore the diversity of genomes in humans in order to find the gene which is responsible for population specific population specific phenotypic expression, disease phenotypic expression, and genetic variance in population specific in india using Whole Genome Sequencing, Whole Exome Sequencing and SNP indel based studies.

Denovo Genome assembly

Genome Information is crucial to understand conserved gene regulatory mechanisms and aspects of body design. So our lab is invested to reconstruct genomes to achieve a reference genome for a specific species using super scaffolding, genome annotation, homology based and gene prediction method.

Simple Sequence Repeats

Microsatellites play an important role in regulatory functions and presence in the coding regions cause neurodegenerative disorders in humans and other possible roles in genome organization. Our goal is to understand how microsatellites are conserving to itself since evolution, emerging roles in genome organization and gene regulation in brief context.
